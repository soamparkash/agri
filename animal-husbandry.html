What is Animal Husbandry?

Animal husbandry is the branch of agriculture concerned with the breeding, feeding, healthcare, and management of domesticated animals. It plays a vital role in providing food, income, employment, and raw materials for several industries in India.

Importance of Animal Husbandry

  • Major source of milk, meat, eggs, and wool.
  • Provides regular income to small and marginal farmers.
  • Supports mixed farming systems.
  • Supplies manure for soil fertility.
  • Contributes significantly to agricultural GDP.

Main Components of Animal Husbandry

  • Dairy farming
  • Poultry farming
  • Sheep and goat rearing
  • Piggery
  • Fisheries and aquaculture
  • Apiculture (beekeeping)
  • Sericulture (silk production)

Breeding Practices

Scientific breeding practices aim to improve productivity, disease resistance, and adaptability. Artificial insemination (AI) and selective breeding are widely used methods.

Feeding & Nutrition

  • Balanced diet containing energy, protein, minerals, and vitamins.
  • Green fodder, dry fodder, and concentrate feed.
  • Clean drinking water at all times.
  • Use of mineral mixtures to prevent deficiencies.

Animal Health & Disease Management

Preventive healthcare is essential for maintaining livestock productivity. Regular vaccination, deworming, hygiene, and timely veterinary care reduce disease outbreaks and economic losses.

Role in Rural Economy

  • Employment generation in rural areas.
  • Empowerment of women through livestock ownership.
  • Income during crop failure or drought.

Government Schemes & Support

The Government of India promotes animal husbandry through schemes such as National Livestock Mission, Rashtriya Gokul Mission, and National Dairy Plan.

Challenges

  • Disease outbreaks and climate stress.
  • Feed and fodder shortages.
  • Lack of access to veterinary services.
  • Low adoption of scientific practices.

Best Practices for Farmers

  • Maintain clean housing and proper sanitation.
  • Follow vaccination and health schedules.
  • Adopt improved breeds suitable for the region.
  • Keep records of breeding, feeding, and production.
