Cultivation, possession, and processing of Opium Poppy is strictly regulated in India under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, 1985. This page is provided only for educational and informational purposes.

General Information

Opium Poppy is a plant species known for producing latex that is used in the manufacture of medicinal opioids such as morphine and codeine. India is one of the few countries where opium poppy cultivation is permitted under strict government control for pharmaceutical purposes.

  • Scientific Name: Papaver somniferum
  • Plant Type: Annual herb
  • General Season: Rabi
  • Climate Preference: Cool and dry

Medicinal & Industrial Uses

  • Production of medicinal alkaloids (morphine, codeine)
  • Used in pharmaceutical pain management (licensed use only)
  • Poppy seeds used in food (non-narcotic part)

Legal Status in India

In India, opium poppy cultivation is:

  • Allowed only with central government license
  • Restricted to selected districts (e.g., Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh)
  • Monitored directly by government authorities

Important Note

Unauthorized cultivation of opium poppy is a serious criminal offense punishable by heavy fines and imprisonment. Farmers must never attempt to grow this crop without valid legal approval.
