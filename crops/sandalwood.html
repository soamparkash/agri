General Information

Sandalwood is one of the most valuable aromatic tree crops in India, known for its fragrant heartwood and oil. It is used extensively in perfumery, cosmetics, medicine, and religious rituals. Sandalwood is a slow-growing semi-parasitic tree with a very long economic life.

  • Scientific Name: Santalum album
  • Crop Type: Tree crop (Restricted)
  • Economic Life: 30‚Äì60 years
  • Climate: Tropical to subtropical

Soil Requirement

  • Well-drained red loamy or sandy loam soils
  • Tolerates slightly alkaline soils
  • pH range: 6.0 ‚Äì 8.0

Host Plants (Very Important)

Sandalwood is a partial root parasite and requires host plants to grow successfully.

  • Short-term hosts: Pigeon pea, cowpea
  • Long-term hosts: Neem, Pongamia, Casuarina
