Overview

Cattle farming (dairy & beef systems) in India is a major rural livelihood activity. India is the world‚Äôs largest milk producer and dairy is one of the largest agricultural commodities ‚Äî providing income, nutrition and employment to millions of rural households. (see sources below for national production figures).

Why Cattle Farming Matters

  • Primary source of milk and dairy products for households and industry.
  • Provides manure for soil fertility and fuel (dung cakes / biogas).
  • Supports livelihoods: dairy directly employs millions of rural families.
  • Supports allied rural enterprises ‚Äî cooperatives, milk processing, input supply.

Major Breeds & Breed Groups

India has three broad groups: indigenous (zebu), crossbreds, and exotic cows. Well-known indigenous dairy breeds include Gir, Sahiwal, Red Sindhi, and among buffaloes the Murrah is famous for high milk yield.

Housing & Farm Layout

  • Shed design: well-ventilated, dry bedding, separate calving area.
  • Space: provide at least 3‚Äì4.5 m¬≤ per adult cow (depends on system).
  • Drainage: sloped floors to remove urine/effluent; regular cleaning.
  • Feed storage & fodder: dry & green fodder storage close to the shed.

Feeding & Nutrition (Practical)

Balanced feeding is the single most important factor for milk yield. A dairy ration typically mixes quality green fodder, dry roughage (straw, hay), and concentrates (energy + protein) adjusted by stage (lactating, dry, pregnant). On-farm fodder production (berseem, maize fodder, sorghum) reduces cost and improves supply stability.

Breeding & Reproduction

  • Use high-quality semen (AI) or well-managed natural service for genetic improvement.
  • Record heat cycles and use timed-AI programs when possible to improve conception rates.
  • Maintain breeding records: date of service, sire, expected calving, and calf details.

Animal Health & Vaccination

Regular vaccination (FMD, HS, Brucellosis control where applicable), periodic deworming and prompt treatment of mastitis/hooves are essential. Calf care ‚Äî timely colostrum feeding within the first few hours ‚Äî strongly improves survivability.

Milking, Milk Handling & Quality

  • Clean milking parlour or area; pre-milking teat sanitation; proper milking machine use (if used).
  • Immediate cooling (if available) or rapid transport to co-op; hygiene prevents contamination.
  • Maintain records of daily yield, milk fat and SNF (for payment & management).

Economics & Schemes

Dairy is often a stable income source. Multiple central and state schemes provide credit, subsidies for sheds, AI & breeding support, and veterinary services through cooperatives and the National Dairy Development Board (NDDB) ecosystem.

Risks & Challenges

  • Feed & fodder price volatility
  • Diseases & seasonal shortages (timely vaccination & fodder planning reduce risk)
  • Market & price fluctuations; need for cold chain and value-addition

Practical Checklist for a New Cattle Farmer (Start-up)

  • Decide herd size & enterprise model (household / small commercial).
  • Identify breed or cross suitable for local climate & feed availability.
  • Prepare shed (drainage, bedding, feed & water points, isolation area).
  • Plan fodder: grow green fodder + reserve dry fodder for lean season.
  • Link with local AI / veterinary / cooperative and register for schemes.

Local Contacts & Useful Links

Example contacts you can add for each district: local veterinary hospital, nearest livestock extension centre, milk cooperative society or dairy union.
