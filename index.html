Indian agriculture is the backbone of the country's economy, employing more than half of the population and contributing significantly to GDP. India is one of the world's largest producers of rice, wheat, pulses, fruits, vegetables, milk, and spices. Agriculture depends heavily on monsoon rainfall, irrigation systems, soil fertility, and climate conditions. Modern farming practices such as mechanization, improved seed varieties, fertilizers, precision agriculture, and digital tools are transforming traditional farming. Sustainable agriculture, organic farming, and climate-resilient crops are becoming increasingly important to ensure food security and farmer welfare.
