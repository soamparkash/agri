What are Fodder Crops?

Fodder crops are cultivated primarily to feed livestock such as cattle, buffaloes, sheep, and goats. These crops are essential for dairy farming, animal husbandry, and rural livelihoods, ensuring year-round availability of nutritious feed.

Growing Conditions

  • Can be grown in all three seasons (Kharif, Rabi, Zaid)
  • Require fertile soil with good moisture retention
  • Regular irrigation improves biomass yield
  • Fast-growing and short-duration crops

Importance of Fodder Crops

Adequate fodder supply improves milk production, animal health, and farmer income. Fodder crops also help in soil conservation and efficient use of agricultural land.

Major Fodder Crops in India

Berseem Lucerne Jowar (Fodder) Bajra (Fodder)
