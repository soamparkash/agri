What is Agro Forestry?

Agroforestry is a land-use system where agricultural crops and trees are grown together on the same land. This system improves productivity, conserves natural resources, and provides diversified income to farmers. In India, agroforestry is promoted to increase tree cover while supporting food security.

Why Agro Forestry is Important in India

  • Improves soil fertility through leaf litter and organic matter.
  • Reduces soil erosion and wind damage to crops.
  • Provides additional income from timber, fuelwood, and fruits.
  • Enhances biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
  • Reduces dependency on single-crop income.

Common Agro Forestry Systems

  • Agrisilviculture: Trees + agricultural crops.
  • Silvopasture: Trees + pasture + livestock.
  • Agri-horticulture: Fruit trees + field crops.
  • Boundary plantation: Trees planted on field borders.

Popular Tree Species Used

Selection of tree species depends on climate, soil type, and market demand. Fast-growing and economically valuable species are preferred.

  • Neem
  • Poplar
  • Eucalyptus
  • Teak
  • Bamboo
  • Subabul

Associated Crops

Crops grown under agroforestry systems should tolerate partial shade and root competition.

  • Wheat
  • Mustard
  • Pulses
  • Vegetables
  • Fodder crops

Economic Benefits

  • Regular income from crops.
  • Long-term income from timber and wood products.
  • Reduced fertilizer cost due to improved soil health.
  • Risk reduction during droughts or crop failure.

Government Support & Policies

The Government of India promotes agroforestry through the National Agroforestry Policy. Farmers receive support for tree plantation, quality planting material, and market linkages.

Challenges in Agro Forestry

  • Initial establishment cost.
  • Lack of awareness among farmers.
  • Market access for timber products.
  • Long gestation period for trees.

Practical Tips for Farmers

  • Choose tree species suitable for local climate.
  • Maintain proper spacing to avoid crop competition.
  • Adopt mixed species for better resilience.
  • Link with local forest/agriculture extension services.
